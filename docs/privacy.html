Ryft

Privacy Policy

Effective July 6, 2026 · WAYLM INC

The short version

Ryft is a local-first journal. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share your personal data. Everything you write, track, and photograph stays on your device. There are no accounts, no ads, no analytics, and no tracking.

Information we collect

None that leaves your device. Ryft has no servers that store your content. The journal entries, checklists, notes, metrics, focus areas, photos, and reminders you create are saved only in local storage on your device. We do not collect names, email addresses, location, contacts, advertising identifiers, or usage analytics.

Permissions we request

Ryft only asks for permissions needed for features you choose to use:

Camera / Photos — so you can attach a photo to an entry. Photos are stored on your device and are never uploaded anywhere.

Notifications — so the reminders you schedule can appear. Reminders are generated and delivered locally on your device; no reminder text is sent to us or anyone else.

You can revoke these permissions at any time in your device settings.

Third-party services

Ryft does not include advertising or analytics SDKs. If we ship an app update, your device may download it from Expo Application Services (EAS), the update infrastructure Ryft is built with. This may involve standard technical information (such as your IP address and app/OS version) being processed by Expo to deliver the correct update. No journal content or personal data is ever included. See Expo's privacy policy.

Storage & security

Your data lives on your device and is protected by your device's own security. Because we never receive your data, we cannot access, view, or recover it.

Retention & deletion

You are in control. Deleting an entry removes it from your device. Uninstalling Ryft permanently deletes all of its data from your device. Since we hold none of your data, there is nothing for you to request us to delete.

Children's privacy

Ryft is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.

Open source

Ryft is open source. You can review exactly what the app does at github.com/Waylm/ryft.

Changes

If we change this policy, we will update the effective date above and post the revised version at this URL.
